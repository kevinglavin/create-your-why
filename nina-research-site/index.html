Why It Matters

Activating the “mattering” mechanism

Graduate attrition has been described as a hidden crisis: roughly 30–50% of doctoral students never complete, most often departing during dissertation, clinical, or practicum stages. For counseling students, those risks intersect with gatekeeping requirements, supervision demands, and a fragile, still-forming professional identity.

When early clinical performance goes poorly and no structured relational support is available, students may interpret the struggle as evidence that they do not belong in the field.

The materials here make a converging case: individualized, faculty-led coaching increases student self-efficacy and the perception that they matter individually — targeting support quality, instructional clarity, and perceived institutional care, the mechanisms that carry the most weight for online graduate learners. Early program data show near-universal student endorsement.

The recommendation: retain the program full-cycle, track immediate surveys, monitor term-to-term persistence, and evaluate and refine for continuous improvement.

Why this matters

Graduate attrition has been described as a hidden crisis: roughly 30–50% of doctoral students never complete, most often departing during dissertation, clinical, or practicum stages. For counseling students, those risks intersect with gatekeeping requirements, supervision demands, and a fragile, still-forming professional identity.

When early clinical performance goes poorly and no structured relational support is available, students may interpret the struggle as evidence that they do not belong in the field.

The documents here make a converging case: individualized, faculty-led coaching plausibly targets the mechanisms that matter most for online graduate learners — support quality, instructional clarity, self-efficacy, and perceived institutional care — and early program data show near-universal student endorsement. The recommendation: retain the program and evaluate it as a retention intervention.
